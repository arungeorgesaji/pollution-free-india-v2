# How Air Pollution Occurs?

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances, including particulates and biological molecules, are introduced into the Earth's atmosphere. These pollutants can have detrimental effects on human health as well as the health of other living organisms and the natural environment.

There are many sources of air pollution, including emissions from vehicles, power plants, industrial facilities, and agricultural operations. In addition, natural events such as wildfires and volcanic eruptions can also contribute to air pollution.

## Primary Sources
- Vehicle emissions
- Industrial processes
- Agricultural activities
- Natural events

# How Can One Prevent It?

Reducing the use of fossil fuels by using public transportation, carpooling, or biking instead of driving alone. Planting trees and maintaining green spaces, which can help absorb pollutants and improve air quality.

## Prevention Methods
- Support renewable energy sources
- Use public transportation
- Plant trees and maintain green spaces
- Support stricter emissions standards

# Measures for Affected Areas

If your area is affected by air pollution, here are steps you can take:

1. **Educate yourself and others** about the sources of air pollution
2. **Get involved** in community organizations working to improve air quality
3. **Support local policies** that aim to reduce air pollution
4. **Reduce your personal footprint** by using sustainable transportation
